Welcome!

I graduated from the University of Toronto with an Honours Bachelor of Science in Mathematics and Applied Statistics, and a minor in Computer Science. My interests lie in quantitative finance, machine learning, and statistical research. You can explore my projects, research and work experience below.

Background

I grew up in a family of doctors. My parents, respected medical professionals, naturally expected me to follow their footsteps, but my interest was more in solving real-world problems. My family's support led me to University of Toronto.

During my undergraduate studies, I undertook an independent study on algebraic numbers with Professor Marina Tvalvazade, and enrolled in shared graduate courses on Mathematical Neuroscience and Mathematical Machine Learning at the Fields Institute of Mathematics.

Soon, I realized I needed to learn more about the subject matter of mathematics, and I am glad that I made a decision to pursue master's studies in Applied Mathematics, although it was a challenging journey. At UofT, I was fortunate to take courses under wonderful passionate professors who each provided me with a deeper insight about the field of mathematics and statistics: Profs. Arul Shankar, Marina Tvalvazade, KE Zhang, Myrto Mavraki, Vasu Tewari, and Rohan Alexander.
